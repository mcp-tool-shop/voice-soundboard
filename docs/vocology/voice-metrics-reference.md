## Voice Quality Parameters by Gender & Age

### Fundamental Frequency (F0)

| Group | F0 Mean (Hz) | F0 Range (Hz) | Source |
|-------|-------------|---------------|--------|
| **Adult Female (20-45)** | 200-220 | 165-255 | Multiple studies |
| **Adult Male (20-45)** | 120-130 | 85-155 | Multiple studies |
| **Older Female (60+)** | 180-200 | Lower than younger | Sciudek 2013 |
| **Older Male (60+)** | 120-140 | Similar to younger | Sciudek 2013 |
| **Children (8-12)** | 250-300 | Higher variability | Literature |

**Key findings:**
- F0 decreases with age in women
- F0 may slightly increase with age in men
- F0 is the strongest predictor of perceived gender

| Group | Jitter Local (%) | Jitter RAP (%) | Healthy Threshold |
|-------|------------------|----------------|-------------------|
| **Adult Female** | 0.5-0.7 | 0.3-0.4 | <1.0% |

| **Older Adults** | 0.6-1.0 | 0.4-0.6 | <1.5% |
| **Pathological** | >1.5 | >0.8 | — |

**Notes:**
- Jitter increases in phonations below 75 dB
- No significant gender difference in healthy voices
- Increases slightly with age in males

| Emotion | F0 Mean | F0 Range | Intensity | Rate | Jitter |
|---------|---------|----------|-----------|------|--------|
| **Neutral** | Baseline | Normal | Normal | Normal | Low |
| **Happy/Joy** | +15-25% | +30-50% | +10% | +10-15% | Low |
| **Sad** | -10-20% | -30-40% | -15% | -20-30% | Slightly higher |
| **Angry** | +20-40% | +50-100% | +20-30% | +0-10% | Higher |

| **Tender** | -5-10% | -10-20% | -20% | -15% | Low |

### Arousal-Valence Mapping

```
High Arousal (faster, higher F0, louder):
  - Angry, Fear, Excited, Surprised

Low Arousal (slower, lower F0, quieter):
  - Sad, Tired, Calm, Bored

Positive Valence:
  - Happy, Excited, Tender, Relaxed

Negative Valence:
  - Angry, Sad, Fear, Disgust
```

## Age-Related Voice Changes

### Vocal Aging Progression

| Age Group | F0 Change | Jitter | Shimmer | HNR | Quality |
|-----------|-----------|--------|---------|-----|---------|
| **20-40** | Baseline | Low | Low | High | Clear |
| **40-60** | Stable | Slight ↑ | Slight ↑ | Stable | Minor changes |
| **60-70** | F↓, M↑ | ↑ 20-40% | ↑ 30-50% | ↓ 10-20% | Rougher |
| **70-80** | F↓, M↑ | ↑ 50-100% | ↑ 50-100% | ↓ 20-30% | Tremulous |
| **80+** | Variable | High | High | Low | Frail |

**Female aging:** F0 drops ~10-30 Hz from 40 to 80
**Male aging:** F0 may rise ~10-20 Hz after 60 (muscle atrophy)

### Voice Aging Synthesis Guidelines

To synthesize an aged voice:
1. **Formants:** Shift down 5-10% (larger perceived vocal tract)
2. **Jitter:** Increase to 1.0-2.0%
3. **Shimmer:** Increase to 4.0-6.0%
4. **HNR:** Reduce to 8-12 dB
5. **Phonation:** Add creaky/breathy quality
6. **Rhythm:** Slower rate, longer pauses
7. **F0:** Adjust per gender (lower for F, slightly higher for M)

## Implementation Notes

### Software-Specific Considerations

> "The difference between results and those from other authors confirm the need to normalize each software to be used."

Different analysis tools may produce different absolute values. When implementing:
- Calibrate against known samples
- Use relative comparisons when possible
- Document the analysis tool and settings used
